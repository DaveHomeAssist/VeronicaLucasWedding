02 — Mood pillars

Keepsake nostalgia

The site should feel like a printed memory, not a generic wedding template. Think Polaroid framing, paper surfaces, and personal artifacts.

  • Visual cue Cream borders, mounted photo treatment, tactile layering
  • Emotion Intimate, personal, collected over time

Dark botanical romance

Build from charcoal, moss, sage, and antique gold. The mood is evening garden, candlelit, earthy, and elegant without feeling stiff.

  • Visual cue Deep backgrounds, restrained glow, foliage-adjacent tones
  • Emotion Warm, cinematic, grounded

Handwritten warmth

Script is an accent, not the whole system. Use it to make the site feel human and romantic, while preserving clarity for guests.

  • Visual cue Script moments, small flourishes, soft micro copy
  • Emotion Personal, affectionate, lightly playful

07 — Brand rules
Do

Keep it intimate

  • Use cream paper surfaces against dark charcoal backgrounds.
  • Let script appear as an accent, not the whole identity.
  • Keep RSVP and details immediately accessible.
  • Use muted gold sparingly for warmth and emphasis.
  • Favor photo treatments that feel printed and personal.
  • Write copy that sounds warm, direct, and guest friendly.
Do not

Break the mood

  • Do not flood the site with bright white or sterile sections.
  • Do not use script for navigation, forms, or body paragraphs.
  • Do not lean into rustic clichés or overdone floral graphics.
  • Do not make logistics harder to find for the sake of style.
  • Do not overuse glow, blur, glassmorphism, or decorative flourishes.
  • Do not let the site feel disconnected from the save the date.
